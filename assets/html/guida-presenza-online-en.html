The method in 4 phases

These are the minimum activities to execute well to build an online presence that actually works.

Phase 1: messaging and positioning 2-3 hours
What to prepare

A clear sentence: I help [target] achieve [result] through [method].

Minimum output: headline, subheadline, and 3 core services.

Mistake to avoid

Writing generic or overly technical copy: visitors do not understand and do not contact you.

Phase 2: minimum page structure 4-5 hours
What to include

Clear hero, services, trust signals, simple contact, and one primary CTA.

Minimum output: mobile-readable page and a frictionless contact path.

Mistake to avoid

Adding too many sections without priorities: the page gets long but does not convert.

Phase 3: form and follow-up 2-3 hours
What to set up

Form with useful fields, post-submit confirmation, automated email, and clear next steps.

Mistake to avoid

A “silent” form: users submit and remain uncertain, trust drops immediately.

Phase 4: initial testing and optimization 2 hours
What to test

Mobile experience, form delivery, email receipt, CTA clarity, and perceived response times.

Mistake to avoid

Publishing without real tests: you discover issues too late and lose leads.

Costly mistakes to avoid

  • Vague copy like “innovative solutions” with no concrete outcome.
  • Too many different CTAs on the same page.
  • A form that is either too long or too poor in detail.
  • No follow-up: people submit and do not know when they will be contacted.

Signs you are moving in the right direction

  • Requests are clearer and more aligned with your target audience.
  • You spend less time repeating the same explanations.
  • Conversations start from concrete goals.

10-day practical plan

Compact version, designed for people with limited time who still want to do things properly.

  1. Days 1-2: define message, target audience, and promised result.
  2. Days 3-4: write core content: hero, services, proof, contact.
  3. Days 5-6: build the page and primary CTA.
  4. Days 7-8: integrate form, email automation, and post-submit confirmation.
  5. Days 9-10: test everything on mobile, fix weak points, and publish.
